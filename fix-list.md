# Fix List — Reviving the Camp Bikes

Parts, consumables, and tools for getting as many of the salvaged bikes
rideable as possible in the few days before DWeb Camp. Most frames are sound;
the real enemies are **flat/perished tubes**, **rust-seized fasteners**, and
**mismatched valve/brake types** on old German city bikes.

> **Quantities below are estimates.** Do the triage pass first (see next
> section), then update the numbers before ordering. Tick boxes as parts arrive
> and as bikes get finished.

---

## Step 0 — Triage pass BEFORE ordering (30–60 min)

Walk the whole pile once and record, per keeper bike, the things that decide what
to buy. Don't skip this — it's the difference between one order and three.

For **every tire/wheel you intend to keep**, note:

- [ ] **ETRTO size** — the `XX-YYY` number on the tire sidewall (e.g. `37-622`).
      Trust this over the inch markings, which are unreliable on old German bikes.
      Common ones to expect: `622` (28"), `590` (26 × 1⅜ roadster), `559` (26" MTB),
      `540` (24"), `406` (20" kids/BMX).
- [ ] **Valve type** — Dunlop/Woods, Schrader, or Presta. These bikes mix all three.
- [ ] **Brake type per bike** — rim caliper / V-brake / cantilever, **OR** rod brake,
      **OR** coaster (back-pedal) hub, **OR** drum/roller. Each needs different parts.
- [ ] **Drivetrain** — single-speed, internal hub (1/8" chain), or derailleur (3/32" chain).

Fill in the per-bike template at the bottom of this file as you go.

## Consumables (order in bulk)

### Tubes & tire repair
- [ ] Tubes — `622` × **~12** (adjust to count)
- [ ] Tubes — `559` / `590` × **~8**
- [ ] Tubes — `406` × **~3** (kids/BMX)
- [ ] **Valve adapter set** (Dunlop ↔ Schrader ↔ Presta) — so a mismatch never blocks a build
- [ ] Patch kits (vulcanizing) × **4–6**
- [ ] Rim tape (assorted widths) × **1 multipack**

### Lubricants & cleaners (the heavy hitters)
- [ ] **Penetrating oil** (e.g. Caramba / WD-40) × **2+ large cans** — by far the most-used item; rust is everywhere
- [ ] Chain lube (wet or dry) × **1 bottle**
- [ ] Degreaser × **1 bottle**
- [ ] Bearing grease × **1 tub**
- [ ] Rags, nitrile gloves, hand cleaner

### Small parts / sundries
- [ ] Cable ties (assorted)
- [ ] Valve caps, generic small fasteners
- [ ] A few generic platform pedals × **2–3 pairs**

## Wear & replacement parts (confirm type during triage)

- [ ] Brake pads — match to brake type(s) found.
      *Do not bulk-buy until triage: rod/coaster/drum brakes use different parts than caliper/V/cantilever.*
- [ ] Brake cables + housing — universal kit × **1**
- [ ] Gear cables + housing — universal kit × **1** (only if derailleur bikes are in the keeper set)
- [ ] Chains — 1/8" (single-speed / internal hub) × **2–3**
- [ ] Chains — 3/32" (derailleur) × **2–3**
- [ ] Master / quick links to match each chain width
- [ ] Spare brake/shift small parts (barrel adjusters, ferrules, end caps)

## Known gotchas on this pile

- **Seized seatposts & stems** are near-certain given the surface rust. Plan:
  soak with penetrating oil early and often, give it time, use leverage gently.
  Worst case, sacrifice the post rather than ruin the frame.
- **Valve mix** — keep the adapter set in the field box so a Presta-only tube
  still airs up a Dunlop-rim bike, etc.
- **Brake variety** — German roadsters often run rod, coaster, or drum brakes.
  Identify before ordering pads/cables.
- **Mudguard clog** — for any bike headed onto the dirt tracks, consider removing
  tight-clearance fenders so grass/dirt doesn't jam the wheel.
- **Prioritize harvesting good tires** from the wheel stash (Schwalbe Marathon Plus,
  Big Apple seen on site) before buying new rubber.
